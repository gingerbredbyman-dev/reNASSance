§00

Before Day 1: set up once

Fifteen minutes, one time. Then you never think about settings again.

Accounts — 10 minutes

  1. Download TikTok, Instagram, YouTube, and Facebook. Make an account on each ("Sign in with Google" is fine).
  2. Use the same name on all four: try NassRises. Taken? Keep "Nass" in whatever you pick.
  3. Profile photo: your face, outdoor light, slight smile. Same photo on all four.
  4. Bio — paste this: "Restarting at 35, in public. Come with me. New videos daily. (Not a trainer — just my journey.)"

Protect the peace — 5 minutes

Do this before your first post, while it's calm.

  • Instagram: Settings → Hidden Words → turn everything on.
  • TikTok: Settings → Privacy → Comments → filter spam and offensive comments.
  • YouTube: Settings → Community → hold likely-inappropriate comments for review.
  • Blocking is housekeeping, not drama. You'll block freely and feel nothing.

The three taps you'll use daily

  • To post: tap the ＋ in the middle → record or upload → Next → caption → Post.
  • To use a trending sound: tap the spinning music title at the bottom of any video → "Use sound."
  • To reply to comments: open your video → tap the speech bubble. Reply to everyone at first.
§01

The Persona

You're not "a fitness influencer." You're the only person on the internet living this exact story. That's the whole strategy.

Brand DNA

  • Name: The RenNASSance — your comeback, with your name in the middle of it.
  • The whole brand in one line: "Restarting at 35, in public."
  • Voice: the funny, honest older brother. You laugh at the machines, never at people.
  • Sign-off: end every video with "Yalla, one more." Every single one. It becomes yours.
  • Never: fake timelines, guru talk, shame.

Three kinds of videos

Every post is one of these. Rotate them and you'll never run dry.

Pillar 1
Sweat

Real workouts, real numbers, shaky last reps.

Pillar 2
Heart

The feelings under the fitness. Letters to Day-1 you.

Pillar 3
Roots

Your food, your culture, your people. (Your aunt's kitchen is a goldmine — see Day 9.)

The one rule

Document, don't perform. Film your actual life — don't act out a fitness video. Real is what people follow now.

§02

The Hopecore Formula

"Hopecore" is the internet's word for videos that make people feel like life is worth it. Yours will be the real kind. Five ingredients:

Anatomy of one

  1. Real footage. Your shaking last rep, your sunrise walk. Never stock clips.
  2. Music that builds. Pick from the app's trending sounds (Setup shows you how).
  3. A few glowing words on screen. "you didn't quit this time."
  4. A caption that zooms out. "life is about the mornings you almost stayed in bed."
  5. An ask at the end. "Send this to someone restarting." Shares are the #1 signal.

Ones only you can make

  • A voice memo to Day-1 you, played over footage of Day-20 you actually doing it.
  • Your aunt's kitchen and your meal prep, one caption: "strength runs in this family."
  • The first time a gym stranger gives you the nod.
§03

Camera Lab

Nobody is born natural on camera. It's reps, same as the gym — and the calendar levels you up a week at a time.

Week 1 — Talk to one person

  • Pretend you're FaceTiming your best friend. Look into the lens, not the screen.
  • Once a day, talk to your phone for two minutes. Never post it. It's a warm-up set.

Week 2 — Outline, never script

  • Write 3 bullet points, then talk like yourself. Reading a script sounds like reading a script.
  • Film sentence by sentence and stitch the honest takes. Nobody owes anyone one perfect take.

Week 3 — Stretch takes

  • On a rest day: talk for 10 straight minutes, no cuts, no posting. After that, 45 seconds feels tiny.
  • Film while walking. Moving feet relax the voice.

Week 4 — Tell a story

  • Every longer video is the same shape: I almost skipped → it got hard → here's why I stayed.
  • Leave one beat of silence before the honest line. It reads as true.

The 30 Days

One card a day. Tap it, do it, check it off. Stars = how much talking-to-camera it asks of you (★ easy → ★★★★★ your finale).

Three words you'll see

  • Hook — your first 1.5 seconds. It decides whether people stop scrolling. Say it first, exactly or in your own words.
  • B-roll — clips of things (shoes, weights, food) with no talking. A beginner's best friend.
  • CTA — the one thing you ask viewers to do at the end.

§05

The Four Apps

Same video, four apps — but always posted fresh inside each app. Sharing a TikTok (with its watermark) to Instagram gets buried; the apps punish each other's logos.

TikTok — where strangers find you

  • The first second is everything. Start mid-action; cut every "hey guys."
  • Raw beats polished here. Trending sounds help; your specific story helps more.
  • Sweet spot: 20–40 seconds.

Instagram — where fans become friends

  • Slightly prettier here: warm light, a good cover frame.
  • Post Stories most days — polls, behind-the-scenes. That's where the friendship happens.
  • Day 23's collab feature posts one video to both accounts.

YouTube Shorts — works like search

  • Boring-but-searchable titles win: "beginner chest press form over 35."
  • Keep it 15–30 seconds — people finishing your video is what promotes it.
  • Teaching videos (Day 18) get found for years.

Facebook — where your age group lives

  • Groups are the cheat code. Be a member first (Day 6), a poster second (Day 17).
  • Post like an invitation, never an ad.
  • Reaction and family videos do their best work here.

The routine

  1. Film once, phone vertical.
  2. Post it fresh inside each app; tweak the first line for each.
  3. This month is launch pace (6/week). After Day 30: 3–5 a week, forever. That beats daily-then-vanish.
§06

What the Top 5% Do

The habits that separate people who grow from people who post. They're already built into your calendar.

Systems

  • Batch: rest days produce 3 videos. Never wake up owing the internet content.
  • Hook bank: a phone note with 10 first-lines at all times. Write 10, film 3, post 1.
  • 20 minutes weekly, 3 numbers only: % watched, saves, shares. Likes are noise.
  • Remake winners before inventing anything new (Day 22).

People

  • Live in your comments for the first hour after posting. Early replies push the video further.
  • Answer one comment with a video every week (Day 19). It turns viewers into regulars.
  • End the same way every time. "Yalla, one more" — until strangers say it back. Then it's a community.
§07

Blind Spots

Things nobody tells beginners — so they can't hurt you.

How the apps actually work

  • Your first 10 posts will get almost no views. Normal. The apps take ~3 weeks to learn who you're for. No judging before Day 21.
  • Shares beat likes. 40 shares outranks 400 likes. Saves are close behind.
  • Never delete a flop. Old videos wake up weeks later, all the time.
  • The first 2 seconds kill more videos than anything else. If you'd swipe past it, trim it.

Protect yourself first

  • Decide your lines before Day 16, in writing: what's on camera, what never is — relationships, faith, home, family. Decide once, calmly. Never decide mid-comment-section.
  • Filters on before you need them (§00). Block instantly, silently, guiltlessly. Never argue on camera — it just promotes the wrong video.
  • Keep your gym's name, your street, and your building off camera. Post after you leave, not live. And your filming spot doesn't have to be home — a parked car is a private recording booth with great sound.
  • On a heavy day, let a friend skim your comments first. Real creators do this. It's strength, not weakness.

Money & rules

  • Use sounds from inside the apps — outside music can get your video muted.
  • If a brand sends anything free, say "gifted" on camera. Every time, from follower #1.
  • Never buy followers. It poisons the numbers brands actually check.

Body & mind

  • 30 days changes habits, not bodies — and saying that out loud (Day 26) is a strategy, not a confession.
  • Rest days are part of the machine. 3–5 posts a week forever beats daily-then-gone.
  • Unfollow anything that makes you feel behind. Your feed is training you too.
  • Been years since a checkup? See a doctor before Day 1.
